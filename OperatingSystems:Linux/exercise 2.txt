4. From the terminal:
 a) List current running services on the system.
 b) Install following services(NB: Some of the services might be installed already):
  i. apache2
  ii. smb
  iii. ftp
  iv. mysql
 c) Configure the services:
  i. for smb, create a directory, touch some files, and create a share for that directory. While configuring the smb service, setup a user who will only be allowed to access the share(read, write, ability to browse through the contents of the share).
  ii. for ftp, create a directory, touch some files, and configure credentials to be used, disable anonymous access.
  iii. for mysql, configure it with credentials of your choice during setup.
 d) Restart the services after configuration.
 e) Stop the services.
 f) Make smb and apache service to start automatically after reboots.
 g) Reboot your system, and view the status of all these services: apache, MySQL, smb, ftp.
 h) Disable auto starting of the services(smb and apache2) at reboot.
